How to use

Three quick steps to your personalized PPGI

Step 1 – Enter Your General Information

Provide your basic personal details so the app can personalize the prediction for you. You’ll be asked to enter:

  • Age
  • Gender
  • Weight (in kg)
  • Waist circumference (in cm)*
  • Birthplace (where you’ve lived for at least 10 years)
  • Blood group
  • Level of physical activity
  • Family history of diabetes
Measuring Waist Circumference
  1. Stand upright and relax your abdomen.
  2. Place a measuring tape around your waist at the midpoint between the bottom of your ribs and the top of your hip bones.
  3. Record the measurement in centimeters (cm).

Step 2 – Add Food Information

  • Enter your serving size (portion in grams), carbohydrates, protein, fat, and dietary fiber.
  • The app includes pre-saved nutrient data for common foods — selecting a food will auto-fill the nutrition fields.
  • If the food is not listed, use the manual entry option to type the food name and nutrition values.
  • For processed foods, use the nutrition label values and the serving size on the label.

Step 3 – Predict and Save

  1. Click the Predict PPGI button to compute your personalized GI (PPGI) and Glycemic Load (GL).
  2. View your results on the page — both GI and GL will be shown along with IAUC values when available.
  3. Download your result as a CSV file (click Download CSV) to save or share.

Note: The portion size you enter is used to compute Glycemic Load (GL). The model itself uses nutrient values per 100 g for predicting IAUC and PPGI.

*Accurate waist measurement improves personalization; follow the guide above for consistency.
